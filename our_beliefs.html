Our Beliefs

Untraditional in our approach but planted firmly in biblical truth, the heart of Praise Church is to reach those who are far from God and lead them to encounter Him in a new and personal way.

  • GOD

    We believe there is one God – the Creator of the universe. God is not a remote, disinterested spectator – he is bigger, better and closer than we can imagine. He exists eternally in three persons – the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit (Genesis 1:26,27; 3:22; Psalms 90:2; 14:1; Matthew 28:19; John 17:3; John 1:1).

  • JESUS CHRIST

    Far more than a moral man or a great teacher, we believe that Jesus is who he said he was - the Son of God. Jesus is God showing himself to us.

    He lived a sinless life, willingly offering Himself up as the ultimate sacrifice to cover the sins of everyone who has ever lived. He arose from the dead after three days, showing his power over sin and death. He ascended to Heaven and will return again to earth to reign as King of kings, and Lord of lords. We believe that Jesus is the only mediator between us and God, and that we can know him personally. (Matthew 1:22,23; John 14:1-30; Hebrews 4:14,15; 1 Corinthians 15:3,4; Romans 1:3,4; Acts 1:9-11; Isaiah 9:6).

  • HOLY SPIRIT

    We believe that the Holy Spirit is present in the world to draw us towards God and make us aware of our need for Jesus. Through the Holy Spirit, God lives in and through us now.

    The Holy Spirit lives in the Christian from the moment of salvation, giving us the power for living, an understanding for truth and guidance in doing what is right. We believe that His power and gifts are available to all believers today (John 16:7-13; 14:16,17; Acts 1:8, 2:4,38,39; 1 Corinthians 3:16).

  • THE BIBLE

    The Bible is God’s word; our ultimate source of truth, the foundation of our beliefs and our guidebook for living. It was written by human authors under the supernatural guidance of the Holy Spirit. Because it is inspired by God, it is truth without any error (2 Timothy 3:16; 2 Peter 1:20,21; Proverbs 30:5).

  • SALVATION

    We believe that God longs to be in a relationship with everyone He has created – but that sin separates us from that relationship. It’s impossible to make up for our sins through self-improvement or good behavior, but God extended His grace to us through Jesus to restore what has been broken by our sin. Only by accepting this gift and placing our trust in Jesus can we be saved from the penalty of sin (Romans 3:23; 6:23; 10:9,10; Ephesians 2:8,9; John 14:6).

  • SECOND COMING

    Jesus is coming again to be reunited with us. We believe in the literal second coming of Jesus, the literal rule of Christ upon earth and the ultimate victory of the eternal Kingdom of God (Acts 1:9-11; Revelation 22:7; John 14:3; 1 Thessalonians 4:13-17).

  • ETERNITY

    Heaven and Hell are real places. For Christians, death is the beginning, not the end. We believe that man was created to exist forever. He will exist eternally separated from God by sin, or in union with God through forgiveness and salvation. To be eternally separated from God is Hell. To be eternally in union with God is eternal life. Heaven and Hell are places of eternal existence (John 3:16; Revelation 20:15; Matthew 25:31-46).

  • SOCIAL ISSUES

    We examine life’s tough issues through the lens of our infallible guide and divine truth: Holy Scripture (II Timothy 3:16-17). We believe God’s word is the ultimate authority on our Church, related policies and personal conduct. As Christians, our beliefs must be consistent with His teaching.

    - We oppose discrimination based on race, age, gender and nationality.

    - We believe all forms of sexual immorality are contrary to the word of God, including sex before marriage, adultery, homosexuality, and pornography.

    - We support the sanctity of all human life from the moment of conception to natural death.

    - We believe Marriage is the uniting of one man and one woman in covenant commitment for a lifetime.

    - We believe all people from every walk of life are in need of God’s truth and must be made welcome to come fellowship and grow with us as we evaluate our lives together.
